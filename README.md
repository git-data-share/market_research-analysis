\# Marketing Campaign Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)



This project is a Power BI dashboard built to analyze customer behavior, campaign performance, and purchase drivers.



It helps understand:

\- Who the customers are  

\- Which campaigns perform best  

\- What drives customers to purchase  





\## Overview



The dashboard is divided into 3 main views:



\- Buyer Composition → Understand customer profile  

\- Campaign Performance → Analyze campaign success  

\- Purchase Drivers → Identify what influences buying decisions  





\## Problem Statement



The business was running multiple marketing campaigns but lacked clarity on:



\- Which campaigns actually drive purchases  

\- Who the target customers are  

\- What factors influence campaign success  

\- Which products and platforms perform best  





\## Objective



\- Analyze customer demographics and profile  

\- Measure campaign effectiveness  

\- Identify top-performing campaigns  

\- Understand product-level performance  

\- Analyze purchase channels  

\- Find key drivers behind customer purchases  





\## Key Questions



\- Who are our customers in terms of age, income, and demographics?  

\- Which campaigns generate the most purchases and revenue?  

\- Which products contribute the most to total sales?  

\- Which platform (Store, Web, Catalog) drives the most purchases?  

\- How does customer income affect campaign acceptance?  

\- Do customer demographics (kids, marital status, education) impact buying behavior?  

\- Which products are most popular across campaigns?  

\- What factors increase the likelihood of campaign success?  





\## Campaign Performance Insights



!\[Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/market\_research-analysis/raw/main/images/campaign.jpg)



\- \*\*Campaign 6\*\* generated the highest purchases (\*\*334\*\*)  

\- \*\*Campaign 5 \& 6\*\* generated the highest revenue  



\- Top revenue product:

&#x20; - \*\*Wine (\~$0.68M)\*\* → highest contributor  

&#x20; - Followed by \*\*Meat (\~$0.37M)\*\*  



\- Purchase platform insights:

&#x20; - \*\*In-store purchases dominate (\~13K purchases)\*\*  

&#x20; - Web and Catalog contribute less  



\- Across all campaigns:

&#x20; - \*\*Wine is the most purchased product consistently\*\*  



\## Buyer Composition Insights



!\[Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/market\_research-analysis/raw/main/images/buyer.jpg)



\- Total customers: \*\*2,240\*\*  

\- Average income: \*\*$52K\*\*  

\- Average age: \*\*57 years\*\*  



\- Majority customers are \*\*Married (864)\*\*  

\- Most customers have \*\*Graduation-level education (1,127)\*\*  

\- Customers mostly have \*\*1–2 kids and teens at home\*\*  



\- Purchase behavior changes with age:

&#x20; - Older customers prefer \*\*Wine more\*\*

&#x20; - Younger groups show more variety across products  





\## Purchase Drivers Insights



!\[Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/market\_research-analysis/raw/main/images/purchase.jpg)



\- Customers are more likely to accept campaigns when:

&#x20; - Income is \*\*≤ $44K\*\*  

&#x20; - They have \*\*kids at home\*\*  

&#x20; - Higher \*\*web activity (visits)\*\*  



\- Product sales increase when:

&#x20; - Income is \*\*> $57K\*\*  

&#x20; - Customers have \*\*fewer dependents\*\*  



\- Key takeaway:

&#x20; - \*\*Income is the strongest driver\*\* for both campaign acceptance and product sales  





\## Key Insights



\- Customer base is mostly \*\*middle-aged, married, and educated\*\*  

\- Campaign success is \*\*not equal\*\* → few campaigns drive most results  

\- \*\*Wine dominates sales across all campaigns\*\*  

\- \*\*In-store purchases are the primary sales channel\*\*  

\- Income plays a major role in \*\*purchase behavior and campaign success\*\*  

\- Customer lifestyle factors (kids, marital status) impact buying decisions  







\## Challenges



\- Uneven campaign performance  

\- Over-dependence on few products (Wine)  

\- Low contribution from digital channels  

\- Limited personalization in campaigns  







\## Recommendations



\- Focus on \*\*high-performing campaigns (5 \& 6)\*\*  

\- Promote \*\*high-revenue products like Wine \& Meat\*\*  

\- Improve \*\*web and catalog channels\*\*  

\- Target campaigns based on \*\*income groups\*\*  

\- Use customer demographics for \*\*personalized marketing\*\*
